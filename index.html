Cardiocascular exercise is recommended by the American College of Sports Medicine (ACSM) to be undertaken for a minimum of 90mins across a week in order to reap the main benefits which include:

Weight Loss

Losing extra weight can aid in reducing the risk of health problems and help in the severity of symptoms. Weight loss can reduce blood pressure and cholesterol as well as reduce risk for diabetes, heart disease, stroke, gallbladder disease, and osteoarthritis.

Improved Lung Capacity

An increased lung capacity helps physical abilities and effectiveness of breathing. This allows for better absorption of Oxygen and elimination of Carbon Dioxide.

Boosting Energy Levels

WHY IS THIS SECTION NOT ALIGNING Boosting energy levels through exercise means a better ability to accomplish our daily goals. It makes us more efficient and in turn our activities will take less time to complete.

Stronger Immune Response

A healthy immune system creates a barrier of prtection that stops antigens from entering the body. And if they do enter body, the immune system produces white blood cells, antibodies and other substances that attack and destroy these infections.

Better Quality of Sleep

Getting quality sleep encourages a constant state of relaxation that helps reduce blood pressure and generally keep it under control which reduces chances of heart attacks and strokes, whilst improving cognitive function and memory.

Improved Mental Health

Exercise helps in becoming physically, emotionally and mentally resilient, resulting in having the tools for coping with difficult situations and maintaining a positive outlook aiding in remaining focused.

Reduces Risk of Disease

Improving lifestyle choices reduces risks of illness and not only protects from long-term disease, but also can improve quality of life in later years.

Stronger Heart Function

A healthy heart pushes out more blood with each pump resulting in more efficient cardiovascular function. This decreases workload on the heart and surrounding vessels and can reduce blood pressure with regular cardiovascular exercise.
